The Romans Road to Salvation

Understanding God's Plan for Salvation

1. All Have Sinned

"For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God;"
— Romans 3:23 (KJV)

This fundamental truth from God's Word reveals that every person, without exception, has fallen short of God's perfect and righteous standard. Our actions, thoughts, and very nature are tainted by sin, separating us from a holy God.

2. The Wages of Sin is Death

"For the wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord."
— Romans 6:23 (KJV)

The consequence of sin is not merely physical death, but spiritual death—eternal separation from God. However, this verse also presents the glorious contrast: God's gracious and free gift of eternal life, made possible only through our Lord Jesus Christ.

3. God Demonstrates His Love

"But God commendeth his love toward us, in that, while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us."
— Romans 5:8 (KJV)

This powerful declaration highlights the immense and unconditional love of God. While we were still His enemies, lost in our sin, Christ willingly sacrificed His life on the cross for us. This act of love provides the only way for us to be reconciled to God.

4. Confess and Believe

"That if thou shalt confess with thy mouth the Lord Jesus, and shalt believe in thine heart that God hath raised him from the dead, thou shalt be saved. For with the heart man believeth unto righteousness; and with the mouth confession is made unto salvation."
— Romans 10:9-10 (KJV)

Here, the path to salvation is clearly laid out. It involves a heartfelt belief in Jesus Christ as Lord and Savior, acknowledging His resurrection, and openly confessing Him. This is an act of sincere repentance and faith, turning from sin to God.

5. Call Upon the Lord

"For whosoever shall call upon the name of the Lord shall be saved."
— Romans 10:13 (KJV)

This final verse extends a universal invitation. Anyone, regardless of their past, who sincerely calls upon the name of the Lord Jesus Christ for salvation will be saved. It is a simple yet profound act of faith and acceptance of His finished work on the cross.
